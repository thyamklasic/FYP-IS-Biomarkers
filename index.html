Abstract

No diagnostic tool currently exists that is fast, accessible, and accurate enough to meaningfully accelerate ischaemic stroke triage before neuroimaging becomes available, despite biological signals entering systemic circulation within minutes of arterial occlusion. This study proposes a unified diagnostic pipeline in which biological knowledge, normalisation strategy, and machine learning validation are treated as explicitly interdependent rather than isolated choices, applied to a plasma protein panel quantified from 30 clinical samples. The findings demonstrate that a biologically informed and computationally integrated approach can extract meaningful and interpretable diagnostic signals from a small biomarker panel, directly validating the multi-biomarker panel design.

α2M — Dominant Discriminator Across All Classifiers
α2M emerged as the dominant feature across LR (0.284), RF (0.457), and GB (0.533) — a broad-spectrum protease inhibitor upregulated during BBB disruption in IS. Its sole achievement of statistical significance on Mann-Whitney U testing (p = 0.011) corroborates its dominant feature importance. All other 8 markers were non-significant, reinforcing the multi-biomarker panel rationale — diagnostic signal is only recoverable through joint multivariate modelling.
Limitations
Sample Size
n=30 — proof-of-concept scale only. LOOCV confidence intervals wide and overlapping; reliable AUC estimation requires 50–100 observations per class.
Missing Metadata
No age, sex, comorbidity or onset-to-draw interval data — α2M prominence may partly reflect demographic imbalance rather than IS-specific biology.
Assay Platform
SNR normalisation produces relative not absolute values — cannot map to published reference ranges or define clinically actionable thresholds.
No External Validation
LOOCV cannot substitute for multisite evaluation across different clinical sites, collection protocols, or heterogeneous patient populations.
Future Work
1
Expand cohort to ≥150 with standardised demographic & clinical metadata; replace LOOCV with conventional train/test split to reduce optimistic bias
2
Enrich discriminative axes — add MMP-9, S100B, D-dimer, vWF, IL-6; adopt multiplex platform to reduce inter-assay variability
3
Validate against haemorrhagic stroke & TIA comparators in an independent multisite cohort before clinical translation
